## Collaboration Guidelines

Here's what you can expect when we work together — a straightforward, transparent process designed to make sure we're always on the same page:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Reach Out With Your Idea">
    Send me a message through any of the contact channels above. Share as much or as little as you have at this stage — a rough concept, a reference image, or a fully detailed brief are all great starting points. The important thing is just to start the conversation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="We Discuss Scope & Timeline">
    Once I've had a chance to review your idea, we'll talk through the full scope of the project together. This includes what deliverables you need, any deadlines you're working toward, and an honest discussion about budget and what's achievable within it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="I Begin Work & Share Progress">
    With everything agreed upon, I'll get started. I believe in keeping clients in the loop, so I'll share progress updates at regular milestones — whether that's work-in-progress screenshots, test builds, or short videos — so you're never left wondering where things stand.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delivery & Final Review">
    Once the project is complete, I'll deliver all the final files or assets in the agreed format. You'll have an opportunity to review everything and request any minor revisions before we call it done. Your satisfaction with the final result genuinely matters to me.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Accordion title="Do you take commissions?">
  Yes, absolutely! Commissions are one of my favorite ways to work — there's something really rewarding about taking someone else's creative vision and helping bring it to life. Whether it's a small, quick asset or a longer-term game project, I'm happy to discuss what you have in mind. Feel free to reach out even if you're not sure yet whether your project is a good fit — I'd rather have the conversation and find out together.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How long does a project take?">
  It really depends on the scope of the work. Here are some rough estimates to give you a general idea:

  - **Small assets or UI elements** (e.g., a single prop model, one screen of UI): 1–3 days
  - **Medium projects** (e.g., a game map, a set of related assets, a UI system): 1–2 weeks
  - **Large or complex projects** (e.g., a full game experience, an extensive asset pack): 3–6 weeks or more

  I'll always give you a realistic timeline estimate upfront before we begin, and I'll let you know as early as possible if anything changes along the way.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What information do you need to get started?">
  The more context you can give me, the better — but here's what's most helpful to have before we kick things off:

  - **Style references:** Screenshots, images, or links to games/assets that capture the look and feel you're going for.
  - **Deadline:** Let me know if you're working toward a specific launch date or have any time constraints I should be aware of.
  - **Budget:** Having a ballpark figure in mind helps us figure out what's achievable and how to prioritize the work.
  - **Description of the project:** A brief overview of what the game is about, who the audience is, and what role this commission plays within it.

  Don't stress if you don't have all of this ready — we can work through the details together during our initial conversation.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can we collaborate on a free project?">
  I'm genuinely open to it! If you have a compelling idea and a serious commitment to seeing it through, I'm happy to discuss a volunteer or revenue-share collaboration. Some of the most fun and creatively rewarding projects I've worked on weren't paid gigs — they were passion projects built with motivated people who cared deeply about what they were making. That said, I do need to balance my time, so I'm selective about unpaid work. Reach out, share your idea, and let's talk — I might just say yes.
</Accordion>
