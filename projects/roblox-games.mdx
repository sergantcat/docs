Building Roblox games is where all of my skills come together into a single, cohesive experience. Each project starts with a creative concept and grows through careful level design, custom 3D asset creation in Blender, and hands-on scripting in Roblox Studio. What makes each game unique is the attention to detail — no two maps share the same visual identity, and every mechanic is designed with the player's enjoyment in mind. Whether it's the satisfying rhythm of a well-designed obstacle course or the sense of wonder you get stepping into a vast open world for the first time, I build games that feel intentional, polished, and genuinely fun to play.

## Development Approach

Every game I build follows a consistent development pipeline that keeps the project organized and ensures the final product meets a high standard of quality.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Concept & Game Design">
    Every project begins with a clear concept document outlining the game's core loop, target audience, visual theme, and key features. I sketch out map layouts, define the progression system, and establish a mood board before a single part is placed in Studio. A strong foundation here saves significant time later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Building the Environment">
    With a solid plan in place, I begin constructing the game world in Roblox Studio. Large structural elements are often built directly in Studio, while decorative props, furniture, and unique set pieces are modeled in Blender and imported as mesh parts. I focus on creating environments that feel cohesive, immersive, and visually interesting from every angle.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scripting Game Mechanics">
    Once the environment is taking shape, I implement game mechanics using Lua scripting in Roblox Studio. This includes player systems (health, currency, inventory), interactive objects, NPCs, game loops, and UI logic. I structure scripts using ModuleScripts to keep the codebase clean and maintainable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Playtesting & Feedback">
    Before publishing, I run multiple rounds of playtesting — both solo and with a small group of testers — to identify bugs, difficulty spikes, performance issues, and areas where the experience could feel more polished. Feedback from real players is invaluable at this stage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publishing to Roblox">
    After addressing all major issues, the game is published to Roblox with a polished game page including a custom thumbnail, an accurate description, and relevant tags. Post-launch monitoring continues for the first few days to catch any issues that slipped through testing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  All projects in this portfolio are built using Roblox Studio as the primary development environment. Custom 3D assets — including props, architecture pieces, and decorative meshes — are modeled in Blender and imported as MeshParts, giving each game a distinct visual identity that goes beyond the default Roblox parts library.
</Note>
